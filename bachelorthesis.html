Bachelor Thesis in Test Automation and Software Quality Assurance

In my bachelor thesis, I explored how targeted test automation can enhance the efficiency, reliability, and scalability of software quality assurance. The project was carried out in collaboration with a company developing mission-critical control center software, where manual testing had previously led to bottlenecks in release processes. The theoretical foundation included an in-depth look at modern test automation strategies, benefits over manual testing, criteria for selecting automation candidates, and the integration of automated tests into continuous integration and deployment (CI/CD) pipelines. I also compared different automation tools and approaches, with a focus on long-term maintainability, scalability, and organizational impact. The practical implementation was based on Ranorex, a professional UI automation framework. Selected test cases were automated and modularized to ensure maintainability and reusability, and then integrated into the companyâ€™s CI/CD environment. This included working with real systems rather than mocked components, which required robust error handling and adaptation to dynamic user interfaces. Key challenges included navigating a complex, object-oriented test framework, working with limited documentation, and resolving inconsistencies in pipeline execution. Overcoming these challenges deepened my understanding of automated test architecture, framework extension, and the importance of stable infrastructure for test reliability. The final solution significantly reduced manual testing time, improved test coverage, and laid the groundwork for further automation within the organization. The project provided valuable experience at the intersection of software development, quality assurance, and DevOps practices.
